**COMPREHENSIVE STOCK ANALYSIS FRAMEWORK**

_A Data-Driven Framework for Investment Decision Making_

# SECTION 1: INVESTMENT CONTEXT

Before analyzing any stock, establish your investment parameters. This context determines which factors matter most.

|     |     |     |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Parameter** | **Options** | **Implications** |
| **Time Horizon** | Short-term: 1-6 months<br><br>Medium-term: 6 months - 2 years<br><br>Long-term: 3-10+ years | Short: Technical analysis priority<br><br>Medium: Blend of both<br><br>Long: Fundamental analysis priority |
| **Risk Tolerance** | Low: Capital preservation focus<br><br>Medium: Balanced approach<br><br>High: Growth focus, accepts volatility | Low: Blue chips, dividends, low beta<br><br>Medium: Mix of growth and value<br><br>High: Small caps, emerging sectors |
| **Investment Goal** | Income: Dividend yield priority<br><br>Growth: Capital appreciation<br><br>Value: Undervalued assets | Income: Focus on payout ratios<br><br>Growth: Focus on revenue expansion<br><br>Value: Focus on intrinsic value |

# SECTION 2: COMPREHENSIVE FACTOR ANALYSIS

## 2.1 Fundamental Analysis

Fundamental analysis evaluates a company's intrinsic value based on financial health, earnings power, and growth potential.

### A. Revenue & Growth Metrics

|     |     |     |     |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Metric** | **Positive Signal** | **Negative Signal** | **Threshold / Benchmark** |
| **Revenue Growth (YoY)** | Consistent double-digit growth | Declining or stagnant revenue | \>10% YoY = Strong; <0% = Concern |
| **Revenue Quality** | Recurring, diversified sources | One-time gains, customer concentration | Top customer <20% of revenue |
| **Market Share Trend** | Gaining market share | Losing share to competitors | Compare vs industry growth rate |
| **Earnings Growth** | EPS growing faster than revenue | EPS declining despite revenue growth | \>15% EPS growth = Excellent |

### B. Profitability & Margins

|     |     |     |     |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Metric** | **Positive Signal** | **Negative Signal** | **Threshold / Benchmark** |
| **Gross Margin** | High, expanding margins | Low, compressing margins | \>40% = Strong pricing power |
| **Operating Margin** | Improving operational efficiency | Declining margins, cost pressures | \>15% = Efficient; <5% = Weak |
| **Net Profit Margin** | Healthy bottom line | Thin or negative margins | \>10% = Good; Varies by industry |
| **Return on Equity (ROE)** | High returns on shareholder capital | Low ROE, capital inefficiency | \>15% = Good; >20% = Excellent |
| **Return on Assets (ROA)** | Efficient asset utilization | Poor asset productivity | \>5% = Good; Industry-dependent |
| **ROIC** | Returns exceed cost of capital | ROIC < WACC (value destruction) | ROIC > WACC consistently |

### C. Debt & Cash Flow Health

|     |     |     |     |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Metric** | **Positive Signal** | **Negative Signal** | **Threshold / Benchmark** |
| **Debt-to-Equity Ratio** | Low leverage, financial flexibility | High debt burden, risk of distress | &lt;1.0 = Conservative; &gt;2.0 = Risky |
| **Interest Coverage** | Easily covers interest payments | Struggling to service debt | \>5x = Safe; <2x = Danger zone |
| **Free Cash Flow (FCF)** | Positive, growing FCF | Negative or declining FCF | Consistent positive FCF |
| **Operating Cash Flow** | OCF > Net Income (quality earnings) | OCF < Net Income (earnings red flag) | OCF/Net Income > 1.0 |
| **Current Ratio** | Strong short-term liquidity | Liquidity concerns | \>1.5 = Healthy; <1.0 = Risk |
| **Quick Ratio** | Can meet obligations without selling inventory | Dependent on inventory liquidation | \>1.0 = Good liquidity |

### D. Valuation Ratios

|     |     |     |     |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Metric** | **Positive Signal** | **Negative Signal** | **Interpretation** |
| **P/E Ratio** | Below industry average with growth | Very high P/E without growth justification | Compare to sector; 15-25 typical |
| **PEG Ratio** | <1.0 = Undervalued relative to growth | \>2.0 = Overvalued relative to growth | &lt;1.0 = Attractive; &gt;2.0 = Expensive |
| **P/B Ratio** | <1.0 may indicate undervaluation | Very high P/B without asset quality | <3.0 for value; Context matters |
| **EV/EBITDA** | Below industry peers | Significantly above peers | &lt;10 = Cheap; &gt;15 = Expensive |
| **P/S Ratio** | Low P/S with improving margins | High P/S for low-margin business | &lt;2 = Value; &gt;10 = Speculative |
| **Dividend Yield** | Sustainable yield above market avg | Unsustainably high yield (value trap) | Payout ratio <60% for sustainability |

## 2.2 Technical Analysis (Short-Term Focus)

Technical analysis studies price patterns and market psychology. Most relevant for short to medium-term investment timing.

|     |     |     |     |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Indicator** | **Bullish Signal** | **Bearish Signal** | **Application** |
| **Trend Direction** | Higher highs, higher lows; Price above 200-day MA | Lower highs, lower lows; Price below 200-day MA | Primary trend identification |
| **Moving Averages** | Golden cross (50 MA crosses above 200 MA) | Death cross (50 MA crosses below 200 MA) | Trend confirmation |
| **Support/Resistance** | Bouncing off support with volume | Breaking below support | Entry/exit timing |
| **RSI (14-period)** | Rising from oversold (<30) with divergence | Falling from overbought (>70) with divergence | 30-70 = Normal; Extremes = Reversal |
| **MACD** | MACD crosses above signal line | MACD crosses below signal line | Momentum direction |
| **Volume** | Rising price with increasing volume | Rising price with declining volume | Confirms price movement strength |
| **Bollinger Bands** | Price bouncing off lower band | Price breaking below lower band | Volatility and extreme readings |
| **52-Week Range** | Breaking to new highs with volume | Making new 52-week lows | Relative strength |

## 2.3 Business & Qualitative Factors

Qualitative factors assess competitive positioning, management quality, and long-term sustainability that numbers alone cannot capture.

|     |     |     |     |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Factor** | **Positive Indicator** | **Negative Indicator** | **Why It Matters** |
| **Competitive Moat** | Strong brand, patents, network effects, switching costs | No differentiation, commodity business, easy to replicate | Protects margins and market share |
| **Business Model** | Recurring revenue, high margins, scalable | One-time sales, thin margins, capital intensive | Determines earnings predictability |
| **Management Quality** | Track record of value creation, aligned incentives | Poor capital allocation, excessive compensation | Capital allocation drives returns |
| **Insider Activity** | Management buying shares | Heavy insider selling | Insiders know the business best |
| **Corporate Governance** | Independent board, shareholder-friendly policies | Dual-class shares, related-party transactions | Protects minority shareholders |
| **Innovation Pipeline** | Strong R&D, new product launches, patents | No innovation, declining R&D spend | Future growth potential |
| **ESG Factors** | Strong ESG practices, low regulatory risk | Environmental liabilities, governance issues | Long-term risk mitigation |

## 2.4 Industry & Sector Analysis

Industry dynamics significantly impact individual stock performance. A great company in a dying industry may underperform a mediocre company in a booming sector.

|     |     |     |     |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Factor** | **Positive for Stock** | **Negative for Stock** | **Analysis Approach** |
| **Industry Growth Rate** | Growing industry (TAM expansion) | Declining or saturated industry | Compare to GDP growth rate |
| **Competitive Intensity** | Oligopoly, high barriers to entry | Fragmented, price wars, low barriers | Porter's Five Forces analysis |
| **Regulatory Environment** | Stable, favorable regulations | Increasing regulation, compliance costs | Monitor policy changes |
| **Cyclicality** | Defensive/non-cyclical (utilities, healthcare) | Highly cyclical (autos, construction) | Match to economic cycle |
| **Technology Disruption** | Beneficiary of tech trends | Being disrupted by new technology | Assess disruption risk |
| **Supply Chain** | Diversified, resilient supply chain | Single-source dependency, bottlenecks | Operational risk assessment |

## 2.5 Macroeconomic & Market Factors

External economic conditions affect all stocks but impact different sectors and companies in varying ways.

|     |     |     |     |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Factor** | **Positive Impact** | **Negative Impact** | **Stocks Most Affected** |
| **Interest Rates** | Falling rates boost valuations, cheaper borrowing | Rising rates increase discount rates, hurt high-debt cos | Growth stocks, REITs, financials |
| **Inflation** | Pricing power companies can pass costs | Input cost pressure, margin compression | Consumer staples, commodities |
| **Economic Cycle** | Expansion phase benefits cyclicals | Recession hurts discretionary spending | Consumer discretionary, industrials |
| **Currency Movements** | Weak home currency aids exporters | Strong currency hurts export competitiveness | Multinationals, exporters |
| **Market Sentiment** | Risk-on environment, bullish sentiment | Risk-off, flight to safety | High-beta, growth stocks |
| **Geopolitical Risk** | Stability, trade cooperation | Trade wars, sanctions, conflicts | Global supply chain dependent |

## 2.6 News, Events & Sentiment

Catalysts and sentiment can cause short-term price movements that may or may not reflect fundamental value changes.

|     |     |     |     |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Event Type** | **Bullish Catalyst** | **Bearish Catalyst** | **Duration of Impact** |
| **Earnings Reports** | Beat estimates, raised guidance | Miss estimates, lowered guidance | Short-term spike, sets new baseline |
| **M&A Activity** | Strategic acquisition, takeover target | Overpaying for acquisition, deal fails | Medium-term rerating |
| **Product Launches** | Successful new product, market reception | Product failure, recalls, delays | Medium to long-term |
| **Legal/Regulatory** | Favorable ruling, patent wins | Lawsuits, regulatory fines, investigations | Can be long-term overhang |
| **Analyst Coverage** | Upgrades, new coverage initiation | Downgrades, coverage dropped | Short-term momentum |
| **Institutional Activity** | Large fund accumulation, 13F filings | Institutional selling, redemptions | Medium-term price support |
| **Short Interest** | Decreasing short interest | High/increasing short interest | Sentiment indicator |

# SECTION 5: BULL VS BEAR SCENARIO ANALYSIS

## 5.3 Base Case (Most Likely Outcome)

|     |     |
| --- | --- |
| **Component** | **Analysis** |
| **Expected Scenario** | What is the most probable outcome? |
| **Fair Value** | Reasonable valuation based on fundamentals |
| **Expected Return** | Annualized return expectation: \___% |
| **Probability** | Estimated likelihood: \___% (should be largest) |

### Expected Value Calculation

_Expected Return = (Bull Probability × Bull Return) + (Base Probability × Base Return) + (Bear Probability × Bear Return)_

Only invest if Expected Return > Your Required Return (opportunity cost)
